Understanding the Air Quality Index

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a system for measuring air pollution. Generally speaking, AQI values below 100 are considered satisfactory where values above 100 are considered unhealthy. The current Air Quality Index was developed in 1999 and is divided into six different categories, with each category representing a different level of health concern. Some of the biggest air pollutants include Carbon Monoxide, Lead, Particle Matter, and Nitrogen Oxide.
